SA 610: Leveraging Insights from Internal Audit for Enhanced Assurance

In today’s dynamic business environment, the role of internal audit has evolved far beyond compliance checks. Internal auditors now provide valuable insights into governance, risk management, and control frameworks. Recognizing this, the Standards on Auditing (SAs) issued by the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) include SA 610 – Using the Work of Internal Auditors. This standard guides statutory auditors on how they can effectively leverage internal audit insights to enhance the efficiency and reliability of their audit engagements.

Could leveraging internal audit insights be the key to smarter, faster audits?

Leveraging internal audit insights under SA 610 not only boosts efficiency but also strengthens overall assurance. Collaboration is the key to smarter audits.

What is SA 610?

SA 610 establishes the principles for statutory auditors when using the work of internal auditors. It acknowledges that internal audit functions can provide valuable knowledge and testing that may be relevant to the external auditor’s work. However, it also lays down conditions, limitations, and safeguards to ensure reliance does not weaken professional skepticism.

Objectives of SA 610

The standard has three primary objectives:

  • Determine relevance: Assess whether internal audit work is useful to the external audit strategy and plan.
  • Evaluate competence & objectivity: Check internal auditors’ independence, qualifications, and credibility.
  • Enhance audit efficiency: Use insights responsibly while retaining full responsibility for the audit opinion.

Key Provisions of SA 610

1. Evaluation of Internal Audit Function

Before placing reliance, the external auditor must evaluate:

  • Objectivity: Independence of internal auditors from management.
  • Competence: Professional qualifications, skills, and experience.
  • Systematic Approach: Sound methodology, documentation, and quality control practices.

2. Areas Where Reliance is Possible

  • Testing of controls.
  • Substantive procedures in low-risk areas.
  • Assessing compliance with policies and regulations.
  • Operational audits that support risk assessments.

3. Areas Where Reliance is Prohibited

  • Making significant judgments regarding materiality, risk, or audit opinion.
  • Fraud detection or areas requiring high professional skepticism.
  • High-risk or complex areas involving significant management estimates.

4. Direction, Supervision, and Review

If the statutory auditor decides to use internal auditors’ work, they must provide clear guidance, closely supervise the process, and thoroughly review the results to ensure consistency with audit objectives.

Benefits of Leveraging Internal Audit under SA 610

  • Efficiency: Reduces duplication and enables focus on higher-risk areas.
  • Deeper Insights: Continuous engagement of internal auditors enriches organizational knowledge.
  • Enhanced Assurance: Collaboration strengthens audit quality.
  • Cost Effectiveness: Optimizes audit resources without compromising reliability.

Challenges and Considerations

  • Ensuring Independence: Avoid excessive reliance on internal auditors.
  • Variation in Quality: Internal audit functions may differ across organizations.
  • Maintaining Professional Skepticism: External auditors must retain a critical evaluation mindset.

Conclusion

SA 610 strikes a balance between efficiency and responsibility. It allows statutory auditors to leverage internal audit insights while maintaining ultimate accountability for the audit opinion. When applied diligently, the standard not only enhances assurance but also fosters collaboration between internal and external auditors, contributing to stronger governance and risk management.

For businesses, this means a more robust audit process. For auditors, it emphasizes the importance of judgment, independence, and careful evaluation of internal audit’s value.
